1. Parties and objective

Client: BDPC Architects. Service provider: CAD Guardian LLC.

CAD Guardian will complete and coordinate three architectural sheets using the supplied Dunn Residence project sources and BDPC drafting standards. Unresolved architectural decisions will be returned to BDPC rather than inferred as concealed conditions or professional conclusions.

2. Included scope

  • Existing floor plan.
  • Proposed floor plan.
  • Site and area plan.
  • LiDAR and source reconciliation required for drafting.
  • BDPC-compatible layers, blocks, text, dimensions, paper space, title block, and plotting.
  • Native DWGs and coordinated review/final PDFs.
  • One consolidated BDPC review cycle.
  • Final issue package, change summary, and unresolved-condition record.

3. Excluded scope

  • Elevations, sections, roof plans, RCPs, details, renderings, schedules, or additional sheets.
  • Structural, MEP, civil, survey, or field-verification services.
  • Permit administration or agency coordination.
  • Independent architectural, code, life-safety, or concealed-condition decisions.
  • Automation, scripts, standards libraries, or software delivered as client products.
  • More than one consolidated review cycle unless separately authorized.

5. Schedule

  • Initial coordinated check set within three business days after authorization, runtime activation, and receipt of complete required dependencies.
  • Overall production target of four to five business days, excluding BDPC review time.
  • Final issue within one production day after one consolidated BDPC review.

6. Client responsibilities

  • Confirm the three sheet titles or accept the listed defaults.
  • Provide separately maintained CTB/STB, font, or title-block dependencies, if applicable.
  • Provide material architectural decisions that cannot be responsibly inferred.
  • Provide one consolidated redline set.
  • Authorize expansion before additional work is performed.

7. Acceptance criteria

  • Three agreed sheets are present and coordinated.
  • Native DWGs open without material file errors and required dependencies are identified.
  • Review and final PDFs plot correctly at the agreed sheet settings.
  • Existing and proposed conditions are clearly distinguishable.
  • Principal dimensions, room labels, and agreed annotations are internally coherent.
  • Assumptions, unresolved source conflicts, and exclusions are documented.

8. Change control

A change occurs when requested work exceeds the three-sheet scope, adds another review cycle, requires new design development, requires substantial source reconstruction not reasonably visible during estimate preparation, or introduces excluded deliverables. CAD Guardian will identify the impact and obtain written authorization before proceeding.

Priority rule: completion of the three drawing deliverables outranks automation. Internal tools are used only when they reduce current-cycle risk or effort without delaying production.

Authorization

Written approval authorizes CAD Guardian to begin the Dunn Residence three-sheet pilot for a fixed fee of $3,200, subject to the scope, schedule, review allowance, exclusions, and change-control terms above.
