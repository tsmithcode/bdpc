1. Parties and objective

Client: BDPC Architects. Service provider: CAD Guardian LLC.

After all kickoff gates, CAD Guardian will prepare and coordinate a three-sheet Dunn Residence drawing package using BDPC-confirmed controlling project inputs and the reviewed point-cloud evidence. Unresolved architectural decisions or conflicting controls will be returned to BDPC rather than inferred.

2. Included scope

  • Existing-condition floor-plan drawing.
  • Proposed-plan drawing reflecting confirmed BDPC direction only.
  • Site and area plan showing agreed footprint, work limits, and context without survey representation.
  • LiDAR and controlling-source reconciliation required for responsible drafting.
  • Standard setup: working set, title block, layers, styles, units, orientation, layouts, viewports, and baseline plot.
  • Check-set PDF for one consolidated BDPC review round.
  • Final native drawing and PDF issue after the included review.
  • Change summary and unresolved-condition record at final issue.

3. Excluded scope

  • Land survey or certified as-built measurement.
  • Architectural, engineering, structural, MEP, civil, zoning, code, permit, or life-safety certification.
  • Scan-registration certification or accepted alignment tolerance.
  • Elevations, sections, roof plans, reflected ceiling plans, details, rendering, or BIM/3D modeling unless separately authorized.
  • Concealed-condition verification, destructive investigation, or field survey.
  • Permit submission, agency coordination, consultants, printing, or third-party fees.
  • Additional or fragmented review rounds and scope additions.

4. Assignment-ready CAD effort plan

Absolute included ceiling40.0 hours

This is the maximum authorized effort for the fixed-fee scope, including standard setup, all three plan deliverables, integrated QA, the included review/final issue, and managed reserve. It is an assignment and capacity control—not time-and-material billing.

Ceiling rule: CAD Guardian will not exceed 40.0 hours under this SOW. If the documented remaining work cannot be completed within the ceiling, work pauses before overrun and the issue is routed to BDPC for written change authorization. Unused reserve does not become additional scope.

6. Schedule and delivery sequence

Check-set target: three business days after all kickoff gates are satisfied. Typical production duration: four to five business days, excluding BDPC review time.

Delivery sequence: standard setup → existing floor plan → proposed floor plan → site/area plan → integrated QA and check set → one consolidated review → final QA and issue.

The production clock begins only after written authorization, start-payment arrangement, confirmation of controlling inputs, and licensed compatible Autodesk runtime or approved remote-workstation access.

7. Client responsibilities and kickoff gates

  1. Provide written authorization of the $3,200 scope, 40-hour ceiling, and one consolidated review round.
  2. Arrange the $1,600 start payment.
  3. Confirm the controlling CAD, design/redline intent, title block, standards, dependencies, written dimensions, and decisions.
  4. Provide a licensed compatible Autodesk runtime or approved remote workstation.
  5. Return one complete, consolidated review response.

8. Acceptance criteria

  • Three included sheets address the confirmed BDPC direction and documented existing-condition evidence.
  • Native files and PDFs open and plot through the licensed production workflow.
  • Confirmed title block, layers, notes, dimensions, layouts, and plotting controls are applied.
  • Every work package has its required handoff and acceptance evidence or a documented unresolved condition.
  • One consolidated review response is incorporated.
  • Unresolved conditions and approved departures are recorded rather than concealed.

9. Change control

Scope additions, extra sheets, additional or fragmented review rounds, missing-input reconstruction, third-party coordination, effort beyond the 40.0-hour ceiling, and services outside the listed deliverables require written authorization. They are billed at $90/hour unless a separate fixed fee is agreed in writing.

Current technical state: native CAD production has not begun. No final native DWG or production PDF exists. Native openability, dependencies, title-block behavior, layouts, plot styles, page setup, and plot fidelity remain subject to licensed-runtime validation.
