05 · BDPC/AEC glossary

Terms worth confirming—not bluffing

Concise definitions for the language most likely to arise around BDPC’s existing-condition, renovation, permit, and production work.

LiDAR

Laser-based measurement that creates millions of 3D points representing visible surfaces. In buildings, it is source data—not automatically a finished plan or BIM model.

Point cloud

A dense collection of x/y/z points captured by scanners or photogrammetry. It is attached as reference geometry and interpreted into drawings or models.

Scan registration

Aligning multiple scan positions into one coordinated point cloud. Poor registration can shift geometry and undermine the reliability of traced plans.

ReCap / RCP / RCS

Autodesk ReCap processes and organizes scan data. RCP is a project referencing one or more RCS scan files used by AutoCAD and Revit.

E57 / LAS / PTS

Common point-cloud exchange formats. Format alone does not confirm that scans are registered, complete, correctly scaled, or ready for drafting.

Scan-to-CAD / Scan-to-BIM

Creating 2D CAD drawings or BIM geometry by interpreting a point cloud. The required detail and accuracy must be defined before estimating.

Existing conditions

The current visible state of the building or space, documented from surveys, scans, field measurements, photographs, and available records.

As-built

A record of what is understood to exist after construction or field verification. The term should be qualified by the source and verification method.

Adaptive reuse

Converting an existing building to a new use while resolving existing structure, code, systems, and historical constraints.

Urban infill

New or renovated development within an established urban area, often with tight sites, adjacent conditions, zoning, and permitting constraints.

Tenant improvement (TI)

Interior alterations that adapt a leased space for a tenant, commonly involving landlord criteria, code review, equipment, MEP coordination, and permits.

Schematic design (SD)

Early design phase establishing concept, major layout, program, and overall direction before detailed coordination.

Design development (DD)

Phase where systems, materials, assemblies, dimensions, and coordination become more defined before final construction documents.

Construction documents (CDs)

Coordinated drawings and specifications used for permit, pricing, and construction. They require licensed architectural review and consultant coordination.

Permit set / permit response

Documents submitted to an authority for review, plus revisions answering official comments. Approval cannot be guaranteed by the production drafter.

AHJ

Authority Having Jurisdiction—the city, county, state, fire marshal, or other agency reviewing and enforcing applicable requirements.

Architect of Record (AOR)

The licensed architect professionally responsible for the architectural documents, design decisions, coordination, and seal.

Code / ADA / life safety

Requirements affecting occupancy, egress, accessibility, fire/life safety, and related design decisions. Drafting support follows BDPC’s licensed direction.

Redline / markup

Review comments placed on drawings or PDFs. The production task is to incorporate them accurately and flag conflicts or ambiguity.

Xref

An externally referenced DWG attached to another drawing. Missing paths, versions, or overlays can break coordination and plotting.

Sheet set

A managed collection of drawing sheets with consistent numbers, titles, references, issue data, and plotting behavior.

CTB / STB

AutoCAD plot-style files controlling printed lineweight, screening, and color behavior. They are required for consistent BDPC output.

Consultant background

Structural, civil, MEP, or other consultant drawing/model used as a coordination reference—not independently redesigned by CAD Guardian.

Investor / leasing package

Decision-focused plans, options, diagrams, and visuals used to communicate opportunity before or outside a full permit/construction set.
