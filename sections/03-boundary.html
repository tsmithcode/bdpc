03 · Transparent capability boundary

What I can own—and what I cannot represent

The strongest position is precise: I can own disciplined CAD production and technical delivery. Brian/BDPC retains architectural judgment, professional responsibility, and final approval.

Ready now

Bounded production under BDPC direction

  • AutoCAD drawing production from an approved source package.
  • Redline incorporation, revisions, sheet updates, and PDF issue packages.
  • 2D existing-condition or as-built floor plans from reliable, scaled source data.
  • Drawing cleanup, layering, xrefs, title blocks, plotting, naming, and file organization.
  • Drawing-package QA, issue tracking, assumptions, dependencies, and change logs.
  • Manufacturing/detail-oriented CAD work where geometry and acceptance criteria are explicit.
Conditional / test first

Feasible after source and standard review

  • LiDAR or point-cloud conversion to floor plans, elevations, or simplified Revit context.
  • Revit production beyond a tightly bounded sample.
  • Whole-building as-built sets, especially with irregular geometry or incomplete scan coverage.
  • Code, ADA, life-safety, zoning, or permit-response annotations prepared from explicit BDPC direction.
  • Complex architectural details requiring design interpretation rather than production translation.
  • Renderings or presentation graphics where a defined quality reference and schedule are supplied.
Hard boundary

Not offered or represented independently

  • Architect-of-record services, architectural seal, stamp, or licensure representation.
  • Independent architectural design authority or final code interpretation.
  • Professional land-surveying, scan-accuracy certification, or legal as-built certification.
  • Structural, civil, mechanical, electrical, plumbing, or fire-protection engineering design.
  • Inference of concealed conditions, assemblies, utilities, structure, or inaccessible geometry.
  • Guaranteed permit approval or unlimited revisions caused by changing design decisions.
  • Fixed-price commitment to an undefined building or incomplete source package.
