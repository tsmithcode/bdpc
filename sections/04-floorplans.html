04 · Brian’s drafting preferences

Residential CAD rules for this pilot

The goal is not merely to trace geometry. The work should inherit the drafting logic Brian trusts: clean, rational, self-checking, and easy for BDPC to continue.

Dimensional discipline

Work to one-half-inch precision

  • Do not dimension residential geometry finer than 1/2 inch unless Brian explicitly directs otherwise.
  • Model accurately enough that displayed dimensions do not conceal avoidable geometric errors.
  • Use chained and overall dimensions that reconcile and permit quick self-checking.
  • Resolve discrepancies between arithmetic, measured geometry, PDF dimensions, and scan data before issue.
Wall convention

Start with the framed-wall logic

  • Use 3.5 inches as the base convention for typical 2×4 framed walls.
  • Increase wall thickness only when the source, assembly, or Brian’s direction supports it.
  • Do not automatically add finish build-up that changes the preferred drafting baseline.
  • Foundation, masonry, and other wall types must follow source evidence; do not infer concealed assemblies.
Openings and blocks

Reuse the established vocabulary

  • Pull door, window, fixture, appliance, and related symbols from the preferred source drawings where practical.
  • Do not invent new opening sizes when an established BDPC or source block already covers the condition.
  • Verify block scale, insertion behavior, layers, attributes, and whether source geometry has been exploded.
  • Maintain a simple approved / reject / needs-review inventory for reusable blocks.
Sheet and graphic standard

Legacy drafting logic + current BDPC presentation

  • Use the current BDPC title block and paper-space organization.
  • Use the preferred legacy dimensioning approach as the primary drafting reference.
  • Match current BDPC fonts, dimension styles, lineweights, plotting, notes, and sheet flow where supplied.
  • Show existing-to-remain, demolition, and new construction using the project’s approved graphic conventions.
LiDAR rule: Treat the scan as measured evidence, not automatic truth. Anchor scale and orientation to reliable dimensions or the City floor plate, compare independent sources, and flag disagreement before drafting over it.
