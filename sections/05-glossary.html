05 · Controlled delivery workflow

From Dropbox upload to issued CAD

Each gate ends with a visible decision. This prevents hidden assumptions, premature drafting, and effort estimates based on incomplete files.

GateCAD Guardian actionBDPC decision / outputStatus
0 · Upload completeWait for all expected source files to finish syncing into the shared folder.Brian confirms the folder is complete or identifies any item still uploading.Current
1 · Access and openabilityInventory files; verify DWGs, PDFs, scans, fonts, xrefs, plot styles, and supporting media can be accessed and opened.Receipt confirmation plus a precise missing/dependency list.Pending
2 · Technical source auditCheck units, scale, coordinate/orientation, block integrity, exploded geometry, LiDAR registration/coverage, drawing standards, and source conflicts.Focused questions, assumptions, risks, and recommended production approach.Pending
3 · Effort estimateEstimate the three-sheet transfer based on verified inputs, sheet content, cleanup burden, and review requirements.Brian accepts, revises, or declines the bounded scope and commercial structure.Pending
4 · Written authorizationLock the scope, outputs, exclusions, reviewer, review cycle, schedule, and acceptance criteria.Written authorization to begin production.Pending
5 · Draft productionCreate the CAD transfer using the agreed standards and maintain an issue/assumption log.BDPC receives the first review PDF and agreed native files.Not started
6 · BDPC reviewIncorporate consolidated redlines and resolve documented production issues.Brian / BDPC confirms corrections and final direction.Not started
7 · Corrected issueReturn the corrected three-sheet package, native files, PDFs, and dependency/source manifest.BDPC accepts delivery or authorizes a separate follow-on phase.Not started
Automation principle: Reusable block extraction, standards scanning, and file-quality checks may support the work, but automation cannot bypass source validation, Brian’s drafting preferences, or BDPC review.
