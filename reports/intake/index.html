Authority hierarchy

  1. Explicit written BDPC dimensions, decisions, authorizations, and redlines.
  2. Current Dunn project CAD explicitly confirmed by BDPC.
  3. Current project PDF, conceptual intent, or design direction explicitly confirmed by BDPC.
  4. Verified LiDAR evidence where visually and measurably reviewed.
  5. Client-safe analytical derivatives with recorded provenance and limitations.
  6. Historical or example material for standards context only.
  7. Inferred values, always labeled and never substituted for confirmation.
Control rule: filename, date, location, format, or presence alone does not establish authority. Conflicts are recorded and returned to BDPC rather than silently resolved.

Documented source-protection rules

  • Read-only sourcesNo confidential source is renamed, moved, repaired, converted, resaved, compressed, or uploaded.
  • Traceable derivativesAnalytical outputs retain private provenance, method, checksum, limitations, confidence, and review state.
  • Native metadataCoordinates, scales, offsets, dimensions, classifications, timestamps, and other attributes remain unchanged unless a separately documented transform is authorized.
  • Licensed-runtime boundaryViewer or open-source inspection is not represented as Autodesk production validation.

Duplicate and conflict handling

Duplicate, historical, or parallel inputs remain evidence until BDPC identifies the controlling source. No content conflict is resolved from recency or naming alone. Exact private duplicate findings stay in the reconciliation record and are not published here.

Privacy boundary

This OS publishes aggregates and reviewed derived figures only. It excludes confidential source files, source-relative filenames, raw scans or source photographs, exact address, exact coordinates, detailed bounds, source hashes, private correspondence, credentials, and private manifests.
